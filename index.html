Australian's Enjoyment of Music

Personal Impacts of Attending Live Shows

The chart to the left shows the percentage of people and how much of an impact attending live music showings had on them. From here we can see how much music meant to Australians, with at least 90% of individuals reporting some level of positive impact towards their emotional well-being.

The live show attendees describe how going to these shows help them deal with stress, help relax, reduce depressive symptoms, and go as far as helping them meet new friends, sort of like a social outing.

Music Listening Frequency by Age Group

A recent survey done on Australians of differing age groups show varying frequencies of listening to Australian music. The results show that most Australians choose to listen to music of domestic origin.

This could imply that many Australiand do enjoy listening to local music. Although a good deal of Australians listen to Australian music often, local music isnt exactly the most dominant type of music being consumed.

Sales of Digital music compared to Physical music in Australia

Over time, the digital medium for music has skyrocketted in popularity, beating physical music sales and practically taking over it's place. From the graph we see how as digital music became more popular, physical music sales would drop along with it. From the graph e can see the only time where these sales were equal to eachother was somewhere during the year 2013, which was still in the earlier years of music being sold digitally.

Another thing to note is how digital sales over time have exceeded even the peaks of physically sold music. One of the biggest factors for this is the accessibility of digital music, especially with it being able to be distributed anywhere given there is internet available. For australians, the rise in digital mediums have helped them discover more songs and music variety in general, leading to a larger music industry.

Sales by Genre

The table on the right shows the percentage of sales of a specific music genre relative to the sale of all music in Australia. We can see clearly that the 2 most popular genres being Rock music and Pop music. Rock being a genre with a long history, and pop being a generally loved genre even outside of Australia, with roughly half of the population mainly listening to it.

What's strange is how over time, the sales in each genre have slowly been converging on these two specific genres more and more, which leads to other genres of music being less popular overall. This could be due to the industry landscape and how since these two genres reign over the others, no artist would want to attemp to make music of those genres, therefore gravitating towards pop and rock songs, in order for their music to appeal to more people.

Sales by Origin

From this chart on the left, we can see the distribution of international and domestic music sales throughout the years. A certain trend with the data present is how throughout the years, the sales of both international and domestic music has stayed relatively the same since the beginning.

A reason for this is could be the close relationship Australia has towards music and the creation of it. Additionally, many australians, and people from other countries alike, also feel a sense of pride having music made from their home country, which is why despite the ever increasing international market, Australia's domestic sales remain intact.

The Future of Music in Australia

The Music Market Based on Past Rankings

Although there are many aspects of the Australian music industry that supports the fact that it is doing well, the truth is, it's been on a steady slow decline over the past couple of years.

The reasons for this decline is uncertain for now, but a very plausible explanation is the digitalization of music media which takes away revenue from almost all non-digital sales made from music, which includes things such as concerts, vinyl records, and even merchandise as well.
